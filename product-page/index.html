Pros and Cons of alloy wheels

Pros:

  • Better looks
    Steel wheels can never look as good as alloy wheels, and the cosmetic upgrade and improved visual impact are the major reasons why so many car owners options for them.
  • Better fuel economy
    Because alloy wheels are lighter than steel wheels, fuel efficiency is increased to a certain extent.
  • Better performance
    Alloy wheels decrease unsprung weight and therefore transmit less inertia to the springs, thus allowing the suspension to follow the terrain more easily, thereby providing better grip. In addition, handling, steering and cornering see a marked improvement.

Cons:

  • More expensive to produce, buy and repair
    The manufacturing processes of alloy wheels are more complex than traditional steel wheels. As a result alloy wheels cost a lot more than their steel counterparts, easily up to 75 to 80 percent more. Steel wheels also cost much less to repair in case of damage.
  • Not as high-strength as steel wheels
    Steel wheels are significantly stronger than alloy wheels and are able to take much more impact before bending or even cracking
  • More attractive to thieves
    Since they are far better looking and cost way more than traditional steel wheels, alloys tend to attract attention of the wrong kind.
